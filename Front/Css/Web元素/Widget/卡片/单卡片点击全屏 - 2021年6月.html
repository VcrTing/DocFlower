History has recorded that he was a sinner in the Dunhuang Grottoes.

I have seen pictures of him, dressed in homespun cotton-padded clothes, with glassy-eyed and timid eyes. He was one of the common Chinese people you could see everywhere at that time. He was a farmer in Macheng, Hubei province. He fled to Gansu province and became a Taoist priest. After several twists and turns, unfortunately, he became the home of the Mogao Grottoes, controlling the most brilliant culture of ancient China. He took meager sums of money from foreign adventurers and had them cart away boxes and boxes of Dunhuang antiquities. Today, the experts of the Dunhuang Academy have to buy microfilms of Dunhuang documents from foreign museums again and again, and go to the magnifying machine with a sigh.

The flood of wrath could be poured upon him. But, he is too humble, too small, too ignorant, the biggest pour is just to cast pearls before swine, for a indifferent expression. Let his ignorant body shoulder all this cultural debt, even we would be bored.
